Description

South Indian biryani is a flavorful, spicy rice dish that combines long-grain basmati rice with marinated meat (like chicken, mutton, or seafood), yogurt, and aromatic spices such as cloves, cardamom, cinnamon, and bay leaves. Unlike North Indian biryani, which often has a milder flavor, South Indian biryani is typically more robust and fiery due to the liberal use of spices, green chilies, and curry leaves. The rice and meat are often cooked together in layers, allowing the flavors to meld beautifully, with regional variations like the spicy Hyderabadi biryani or coconut-infused Tamil Nadu-style biryani adding unique local twists. It's commonly served with cooling raita and tangy salan (curry) on the side.

Ingredients

  • Marinade For Chicken Biryani
    • 1.1lbs chicken
    • 3 tablespoons of plain yogurt
    • 1¼ tablespoons of ginger garlic paste
    • ½ to ¾ teaspoons of salt
    • 1¼ teaspoon of tumeric
    • ½ to 1 teaspoon of red chilli powder
    • ½ to 1 tablespoon of garam masala
    • 1 tablespoon of lemon juice
  • Whole Spices
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 4 green cardamoms
    • 6 cloves
    • 1 inch of cinnamon
    • 1 star anise
    • ¾ teaspoon of shahi jeera
    • 1 strand of mace
  • Other Ingredients
    • 2 cups of basmati rice
    • 2 tablespoons of ghee
    • 1 large onion
    • ¼ to ½ cups of mint leaves
    • 1 green chili
    • ¼ cup of plain yogurt
    • ¼ to ½ teaspoon of red chilli
    • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
    • 3 cups of water
    • ¾ tablespoon of salt
    • 2 tablespoons of fried onions
    • 1 pinch of saffron

Steps

  1. Preparation
    1. Add yogurt, ginger garlic paste, salt, garam masala, tumeric, lemon juice, and red chilli powder into a bowl.
    2. Mix it up and add more salt and spice if needed.
    3. Cut slits into your chicken. Then add it to your marinade and mix it well. Cover the chicken and set it aside for 1 hour until overnight.
    4. Rinse the basmati rice 3 times and let it soak for 30 minutes. Drain it to a colander after.
  2. Making the Chicken biryani
    1. Heat ghee or oil in a pot or pressure cooker.
    2. Add whole spices.
    3. When the spices begin to sizzle, add your onions and fry them evenly. Stir often until the fried onions are uniformly light brown.
    4. Add chicken and saute until pale for 5 minutes on a medium heat.
    5. Cover and cooke on a low flame until the chicken is soft cooked or tender. Saute to evaporate any excess moisture in the pot.
    6. Add yogurt, half of the chopped mint leaves, slit green chili, red chilli powder, and garam masala.
  3. Layering
    1. Mix everything well and layer the chicken evenly at the bottom.
    2. Spread drained rice in a layer over the chicken.
    3. In a separate pot pour some water.
    4. Add salt and mix. The water must be slightly salty in order to bring to a rolling boil.
    5. Pour 2 cups of this water across the sides of the cooker. Gently pour the rest on tip of the rice.
    6. Level the rice if needed. Sprinkle the rest of the mint leaves. Add fried onions and saffron soaked milk.
    7. Set the cooker flame to medium high. Cover and place the whistle. Cook until you hear a whistle.
    8. Rest until the pressure drops naturally.
    9. Finally serve your dish.
